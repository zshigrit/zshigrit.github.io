Zheng Shi, Ph.D.

Research Scientist
Institute for Environmental Genomics, University of Oklahoma

I am an ecosystem ecologist focused on advancing predictive understanding of terrestrial carbon cycling under global change. My research integrates long-term field experiments, large-scale data synthesis, process-based modeling, and emerging machine learning approaches to improve mechanistic representation of plant–soil–microbial interactions in Earth system models. By bridging empirical observations with model development, my work aims to reduce structural and parametric uncertainties in land carbon cycle projections and enhance the reliability of climate–carbon feedback predictions.

I received my Ph.D. in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology program at the University of Oklahoma and completed postdoctoral training at the University of California, Irvine and Oak Ridge National Laboratory. I am currently a Research Scientist at the Institute for Environmental Genomics, University of Oklahoma.

Research directions

  • Field experiment: long-term temporal responses of plant and soil carbon cycle to environmental change
  • Data synthesis: cross-biome responses of plant and soil carbon dynamics to environmental change
  • Mechanistic modeling: mechanisms and uncertainties in ecosystem and soil carbon response to climate change
  • Machine learning: combining process-based models with machine learning for mechanisms identification and extrapolation

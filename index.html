Data-driven understanding and prediction of ecosystem dynamics

The EcoDynamics lab studies terrestrial ecosystem biogeochemical cycling using field experiments, data synthesis, mechanistic modeling, and machine learning. Our research focuses on plant and soil carbon dynamics, microbial processes, and improving Earth system model predictions under climate change.

A major goal of the lab is to reduce uncertainty in land carbon cycle projections by integrating experimental benchmarks (e.g., warming and precipitation manipulations), global datasets including radiocarbon constraints, and model evaluation frameworks. We develop tools for benchmarking and diagnosis of land models and explore hybrid approaches that combine process-based models with modern machine learning methods.

Current research directions

  • Field experiment: long-term temporal responses of plant and microbial communities and carbon cycle to environmental change
  • Data synthesis: spatial responses of plant and soil carbon dynamics to environmental change
  • Mechanistic modeling: mechanisms and uncertainties in ecosystem and soil carbon response to climate change
  • Machine learning: combining process-based models with machine learning for mechanisms identification and extrapolation

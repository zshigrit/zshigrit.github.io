Ecosystem modeling and soil carbon dynamics

The EcoDynamics Lab studies terrestrial ecosystem carbon cycling using field experiments, data synthesis, mechanistic modeling, and machine learning. Our work focuses on soil carbon dynamics, microbial processes, and improving Earth system model predictions under climate change.

A major goal of the lab is to reduce uncertainty in land carbon cycle projections by integrating experimental benchmarks (e.g., warming and precipitation manipulations), global datasets (including radiocarbon constraints), and model evaluation frameworks.

We develop and apply tools for benchmarking and diagnosis of land models, and we explore hybrid approaches that combine process-based models with modern ML methods for prediction, parameter estimation, and uncertainty quantification.

Current focus

  • Radiocarbon-informed soil carbon turnover and age distributions
  • Benchmarking land carbon cycle models with experiments and observations
  • Mechanisms and uncertainties in soil carbon response to climate change
  • Hybrid modeling: combining process models with machine learning

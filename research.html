Research

Our research integrates experiments, synthesis datasets, mechanistic modeling, and machine learning to understand and predict terrestrial ecosystem carbon cycling under global change.

Soil carbon dynamics and radiocarbon

We study soil carbon age distributions, turnover, and persistence using radiocarbon measurements, synthesis databases, and models that link soil processes to climate and ecosystem change.

  • Radiocarbon-informed constraints on soil carbon turnover
  • Mechanisms controlling soil carbon persistence and stability
  • Data–model integration to quantify uncertainty

Earth system model benchmarking

We develop experimental-data-informed benchmarking and evaluation workflows to diagnose and reduce uncertainties in simulated soil carbon change and land carbon cycle feedbacks in Earth system models.

  • Benchmarking frameworks for land carbon cycle processes
  • Cross-model comparison and uncertainty attribution
  • Linking model behavior to observations and experiments

Machine learning in ecosystem modeling

We combine machine learning with mechanistic models to improve prediction, parameter estimation, and model evaluation, with an emphasis on reproducibility and interpretability.

  • ML-enabled benchmarking and model development
  • Hybrid modeling and emulator approaches
  • Uncertainty quantification and out-of-sample prediction

Microbial processes and biogeochemistry

We investigate how microbial community processes and functional traits influence decomposition, nutrient constraints, and soil carbon responses to warming and precipitation change.

  • Microbial controls on decomposition and carbon use efficiency
  • Warming and precipitation extremes in grassland systems
  • Coupled carbon–nitrogen modeling perspectives
